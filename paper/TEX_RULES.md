TeX editing rules for this paper

1. Every new sentence must start on a new source line.
2. Do not insert extra blank lines between sentences just for wrapping.
3. Prefer editing the existing canonical `.tex` files instead of creating new TeX files.
4. Do not use compound adjectives of the form X-specific (e.g. PLN-specific, count-specific, sample-specific) or X-based (e.g. PLN-based, simulation-based, model-based). Rephrase to avoid them.
5. Do not use the word "context". Rephrase to avoid it.
6. Do not use compound adjectives of the form X-centered (e.g. reproducibility-centered, data-centered). Rephrase to avoid them.
7. Do not use compound adjectives of the form X-derived (e.g. bootstrap-derived, model-derived). Rephrase to avoid them.
8. Do not use the word "via". Rephrase to avoid it.
9. Do not use the word "perturbation" or any of its forms (perturb, perturbed, perturbations). Rephrase to avoid them.
10. Prefer the word "setting" instead of "regime".
11. Keep following these rules for all future manuscript edits unless explicitly changed.
